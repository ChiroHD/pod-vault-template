## Summary

Riley's 1:1 with Dr. C, statement-redesign as the lead topic. Riley walked the V2 layout mockups, the portal-preview format options, and the email-template direction. Dr. C approved the layout, locked the portal preview as embedded PDF (one render, not two), and gave a clear "branded, not plain" direction on the email template. Two follow-up decisions recorded over the next week.

## Key Decisions

1. **Layout V2 approved as designed.** Three-column body (charges / payments / credits), single "amount due" footer, practice header. Ship as drawn.
2. **Portal preview = read-only embedded PDF.** Same file the staff sees and the patient receives. No HTML render. Recorded as [[2026-04-28 Patient portal preview — read-only embedded PDF]].
3. **Email template = branded.** Practice logo, practice contact info, configurable note. Not plain. Recorded as [[2026-05-05 Email template — branded vs plain]] after Riley showed both options in the next 1:1.

## Key Discussion Points

### Layout Walkthrough

Riley showed the Figma mockups side-by-side with a current production statement. Dr. C focused on three things:

- **"Amount due" hierarchy.** Approved the single bolded footer line. Specifically pushed back on Riley's earlier draft that had "amount due" repeated at top and bottom: "Once. At the bottom. Patient knows where to look."
- **Column ordering.** Charges, then payments, then credits. Dr. C: "Money in / money out / where they stand. That's the patient's mental model."
- **Date formatting.** Dr. C asked for explicit `Mar 14, 2026` over `03/14/2026`. Less ambiguity for older patients.

### Portal Preview Format

Riley had two mocks: HTML render (with inline "Pay Now" button) vs embedded PDF (read-only). Dr. C's response, paraphrased from Riley's notes:

> "The patient should see exactly what the staff sent. If it's a PDF in the email, it's the same PDF in the portal. Don't have two renders that can drift. Pay-now-from-portal is its own feature — don't bundle."

Two design questions resolved in one sentence: single render (PDF), no pay-now (separate feature). This is consistent with Dr. C's broader pattern of refusing to bundle features that should ship on their own merits — see [[Stakeholder Communication — Dr. C]].

### Email Template Question (Surfaced, Not Yet Resolved)

Riley raised the question of email body design — plain vs branded — without committing. Dr. C asked to see both options. Riley scheduled a follow-up for 5/5 to walk through mockups.

(Resolved 5/5 — branded with practice logo. See [[2026-05-05 Email template — branded vs plain]].)

### Beta Cohort

Riley flagged that beta planning was the next major decision. Dr. C asked for the plan to be done by mid-May with names, agreement signed, and onboarding logistics owned by Maya. Not a hard commitment from Dr. C — broad release sign-off would be in their 1:1 after beta wraps.
