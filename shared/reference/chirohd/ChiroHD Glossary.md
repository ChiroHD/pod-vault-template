# ChiroHD Glossary

## A

- **Active User (no calendar)**: A user within a location who does not have a calendar enabled; appears in the Active Users section but does not have a visible tab on the scheduling calendar.
- **Add Included Services Only**: A fee schedule population method where only the specific services included in a care package are manually added and priced, rather than importing all services. Recommended for simplicity.
- **Appointment Note**: A free-text field available when creating or editing an appointment that allows staff to attach contextual information to that specific visit.
- **Appointment Status**: A calendar icon indicator showing whether an appointment is scheduled or completed.
- **Arrival Queue**: A provider-specific list of patients who have arrived and are waiting to be seen; visible on the SOAP notes screen, broken out by provider so each works from their own queue.
- **Arrive / Arrived Status**: The appointment state triggered when a patient checks in, visually indicated on the calendar. Can be triggered manually by staff or via a self-service check-in device.
- **Autocomplete Appointments**: A settings-based feature that automates the appointment completion step when all required conditions are met.

## B

- **Billing Address Toggle**: A button in Office Info settings that auto-populates the billing address field when it matches the physical office address.
- **Blue Bar Indicator**: A visual marker displayed on the side of an appointment tile on the calendar signaling that a note has been added to that appointment.
- **Blue Patient Notes Box**: A free-text field on the Patient Snapshot used to record payment-related or other contextual notes about a patient.
- **Bulk Scheduler**: A tool that allows staff to schedule a patient for multiple future appointments at once using a system-level template.

## C

- **Calendar Order**: A numeric field assigned per provider that determines the left-to-right sequence in which provider calendar tabs appear in the scheduling view.
- **Calendar Settings (per-user)**: Individual user-level configuration options that control how the logged-in user sees and interacts with the calendar, including view type, icon display preferences, and appointment visibility filters.
- **Calendar User**: A user with a calendar enabled in ChiroHD; appears under Calendar Users in Settings → Users and has a visible tab on the scheduling screen.
- **Card Vault**: A secure card storage feature within Fortis that saves a patient's payment card information for future transactions.
- **Care Plan**: A defined course of treatment for a patient, often tied to a set number of visits, a fee schedule, and automated reminders via Patient Alert Templates.
- **Case**: A patient record grouping that organizes visits, charges, and settings (including Default Charges and fee schedules) under a specific care context.
- **Case Type**: A classification selected at the time of scheduling (e.g., Cash, PI, Pediatric) that determines default appointment types and billing presets for a patient.
- **Charge Frequency**: A setting on a service line within Default Charges that controls how often the system automatically applies a charge (e.g., Every Visit).
- **Check-In Device**: A self-service kiosk or tablet where patients enter their phone number, confirm their identity, and check themselves in for an appointment.
- **ChiroHD Academy**: ChiroHD's official onboarding and training platform, hosted on Thinkific, designed to guide new users through full practice setup and system use.
- **ChiroHD Version Bar**: A clickable element in the bottom-left corner of the home screen displaying the current ChiroHD version number; serves as the entry point to the Release Notification Dashboard.
- **Color (Calendar Slot)**: A visual indicator assigned to calendar time slots to signal appointment type or special scheduling rules to front desk staff.
- **Columns**: A provider schedule setting defining the number of simultaneous patients that can be scheduled in a given time slot.
- **Complete Appointment**: An appointment status applied when all three conditions are met — an appointment exists on the calendar, charges have been entered, and a SOAP note has been submitted. Displays as a grayed-out appointment on the calendar.
- **CPT Codes**: Current Procedural Terminology codes entered into ChiroHD for billing and claims purposes; represent the services rendered to patients.

## D

- **Day 1 (New Patient Appointment)**: The initial visit workflow in ChiroHD, including scheduling, digital paperwork intake, charge and payment entry, and SOAP note creation.
- **Day 2 (Report of Findings)**: ChiroHD's term for the second patient appointment, where the provider reviews findings, presents a care plan, and the patient may be bulk scheduled for future visits.
- **Day 3**: An ongoing corrective care visit following the Report of Findings; follows the same general workflow as subsequent visits with default charges potentially applied.
- **Default Billing Provider**: A required Office Info setting that assigns the provider used for billing patients at a location; must be configured for billing to function.
- **Default Calendar Provider**: A required Office Info setting that assigns the default provider used for patient scheduling at a location.
- **Default Charges**: Pre-configured charges defined on a patient's case that automatically apply to the patient's ledger when the provider submits a SOAP note on the same day as the appointment.
- **Default Charges Tab**: A tab within a patient's case where services included in a care package are defined, including discounted charge amounts, write-off amounts, write-off reasons, and charge frequency.
- **Default Owning User**: A required Office Info setting that must be assigned for scheduling and billing to function at a location.
- **Digital Submissions**: Patient-completed intake paperwork submitted electronically (e.g., via check-in device) that automatically populates into the patient's Documents tab in ChiroHD.
- **Documents Tab**: A section of the patient profile where digitally submitted intake forms and other patient documents are stored.
- **Down Arrow Menu**: A contextual menu accessible on a calendar appointment tile that provides actions including editing the appointment.
- **Drop-Down Reschedule**: A menu-driven rescheduling method accessed via the appointment's arrow menu, used when drag-and-drop is insufficient (e.g., different day or different provider in Tab View).
- **Drag-and-Drop Rescheduling**: A method to reschedule an appointment by clicking and dragging the appointment block to a new time slot directly on the calendar.

## E

- **EIN (Employer Identification Number)**: A 9-digit tax identification number used in insurance billing; entered without dashes in ChiroHD provider records.
- **Exception Hours**: A one-day override to a provider's normal schedule (e.g., for early closures or holidays) that does not affect any other scheduled day.

## F

- **Fee Schedule**: A system-level pricing template that overrides standard service rates for specific patients or care packages; must be built at the system level before being attached to a patient case.
- **Fortis**: An integrated merchant services and payment processing provider that connects directly within ChiroHD to handle card transactions.
- **Franchise**: A multi-location network supported by ChiroHD, managed at the System Level where administrators can oversee and configure all locations.
- **Future Schedule (Upcoming Schedule Changes)**: A new provider schedule set to activate on a specific future date, replacing the current ongoing schedule from that date forward.

## H

- **Hide/Show Completed Appointments**: A calendar toggle that filters completed appointments out of or back into the calendar view without deleting them.
- **Hour Intervals**: The time labels displayed down the left side of the calendar; all providers at a location must share the same hour interval setting to avoid system conflicts.

## I

- **Import All Services**: A fee schedule population method that imports every service in the system, after which charge amounts are edited for services included in the care package.
- **Intruded Status (Green Appointment)**: A calendar color state indicating that a SOAP note has been submitted by the provider for that appointment.
- **Inventory**: Products and supplies managed within ChiroHD, configured during initial practice setup and available to add to patient transactions.

## L

- **Language Preference (Icon)**: A calendar appointment icon that indicates a patient's language preference.
- **Ledger**: The financial record associated with a patient's case, showing charges, payments, write-offs, and running balance or credit.
- **Legal Name**: The official legal business name of a practice; can be toggled to display on reports instead of the standard location name.
- **Location Email**: The email address tied to a location in Office Info; used as the sender or reference address for new patient welcome emails.
- **Location Level**: The individual clinic view within ChiroHD that a user accesses upon logging into a specific location; contains location-specific integrations, providers, utilities, calendar, and local settings.
- **Location Name**: The primary display name for an office location, shown throughout the system.
- **Location Setup**: The process of configuring one or more practice locations within ChiroHD, covering physical address, contact info, billing details, and operational settings per location.
- **Location-Level User**: A user created within a specific location in ChiroHD; only location-level users can have calendars enabled.

## M

- **Minute Time Blocks**: The increment size used when building provider hours on the schedule grid (e.g., 10, 15, or 30 minutes); affects how hours are constructed but not how appointments are booked.
- **Monthly Auto-Debit Care Package**: A care package payment structure where a patient is billed in recurring installments (e.g., $200/month × 3 months) rather than paying the full amount upfront.
- **MPI (Master Provider Identifier)**: ChiroHD's label for the NPI Number field in provider records; the 10-digit National Provider Identifier required for insurance billing.
- **Multi-Location Support**: ChiroHD's capability to support practices operating across multiple locations, with data segmentation and reporting managed at the System Level.

## N

- **New Patient Email Template**: An automated email sent to a patient when their first appointment is scheduled, if enabled under Settings → Office → Office Configuration.
- **New Transaction**: A button available on the patient profile that opens the Transaction Module to add charges or payments.
- **NPI Number Type 2**: The location-level National Provider Identifier (as opposed to the individual provider NPI); configured in Office Info settings and required for insurance claims.

## O

- **Office Ally**: An insurance claims clearinghouse integrated with ChiroHD; requires the X12 file format to be enabled in Office Info settings.
- **Office Ally X12 Enabled**: An Office Info toggle that ensures insurance claims are generated in the X12 file format required by the Office Ally clearinghouse.
- **Office Configuration**: The central settings hub within ChiroHD (Settings → Office → Office Configuration) where the majority of practice-level preferences and operational details are managed.
- **Office Info**: The foundational settings page for each practice location in ChiroHD, containing fields for location name, billing details, default providers, NPI numbers, logos, and other core configuration.
- **Opening Date**: A field in Office Info recording the date a location officially opened; used for record-keeping purposes.

## P

- **Patient Alert Template**: A pre-built, reusable alert schedule tied to a care plan that automatically reminds staff to perform specific actions at designated visit milestones (e.g., schedule a scan on visit 5).
- **Patient Mode**: A SOAP note screen view focused on an individual patient's record.
- **Patient Snapshot**: A summary panel displaying a patient's current status, including alerts, charges, payment information, and future appointments; visible to front desk staff upon patient check-in.
- **Patient Snapshot Panel**: A left-side panel in ChiroHD that, when open, blocks access to the ChiroHD Version Bar; must be cleared before the version bar is accessible.
- **Placeholder / Fake User**: A location-level user created without login credentials, used solely to enable a calendar for a provider who already has a system-level login.
- **Practice Preferences**: Global and location-level settings that control how the practice operates within ChiroHD, including scheduling rules, billing defaults, and workflow behaviors.
- **Prepay Care Package**: A care package structure where a patient pays the full discounted package amount upfront, creating a ledger credit that is drawn down as each visit is rendered.
- **Prepay Credit**: The positive ledger balance created when a patient pays upfront for a care package; decreases as services are applied at each visit.
- **Preset (Charges)**: Pre-configured charge groupings (e.g., "Facebook Special," "Day 2") that auto-populate charges on the transaction screen when selected.
- **Provider Hours**: The normal, recurring schedule defining which time slots are available on a provider's calendar.
- **Provider Mode**: A SOAP note screen view that shows an arrival queue of all waiting patients for a given provider.
- **Provider Type**: A classification assigned to a calendar user (e.g., Chiropractor, Stretch Therapist) that must match appointment type configuration; appointments can only be scheduled with a provider whose type matches the appointment type.
- **Provider View**: A calendar layout that displays multiple provider calendars side by side on a single screen; supports the most flexible drag-and-drop rescheduling options.
- **Push Pin**: A calendar settings option that allows a user to set a default calendar tab to display first, overriding the calendar order setting.

## Q

- **Quick Button (Dollar Icon)**: A shortcut labeled "Transaction" available on the calendar appointment view for fast access to the Transaction Module.

## R

- **Release Notification**: A system-generated announcement delivered inside ChiroHD when new features or bug fixes are deployed; appears as a full-screen popup on the first login after a release.
- **Release Notification Dashboard**: A dedicated view within ChiroHD listing all past and current release notifications; accessible via the ChiroHD Version Bar in the bottom-left corner of the home screen.
- **Reminder Status (Icon)**: A calendar appointment icon showing a thumbs up (confirmed) or X (denied) to indicate whether a patient responded to their appointment reminder.
- **Rendering Provider**: The clinician who performed the service; must be linked to both a ChiroHD user account and a provider record for insurance claims to generate correctly.
- **Report of Findings**: See *Day 2*; the second appointment where the provider reviews clinical findings with the patient and presents a care plan.

## S

- **Short Name**: An abbreviated version of a location name used in certain areas of the ChiroHD interface.
- **SOAP Note (Icon)**: A sticky note icon displayed on a calendar appointment block indicating that a SOAP note has been entered for that visit.
- **SOAP Note / SOAP Notes Screen**: The clinical documentation interface in ChiroHD where providers enter subjective, objective, assessment, and plan information for a patient visit.
- **Split Payment (Fortis)**: A Fortis-level feature that mirrors ChiroHD's Transfer function, allowing a single payment to be distributed across multiple patient accounts.
- **System Level**: The top-tier administrative view of ChiroHD where administrators can see and manage all locations across a franchise or network; contains system-wide settings, insurance/third-party payers, and global configurations.
- **System Level Setup Playlist**: A prerequisite training playlist covering system settings, insurance, and third-party payers at the network/franchise level.
- **System-Level User**: A user configured at the system (top) level, typically for system administrators; cannot have a calendar enabled at the system level.

## T

- **Tab Color**: An optional color setting for each provider's calendar tab; defaults to white if not configured.
- **Tab View**: A calendar layout where each provider's schedule appears on a separate tab; drag-and-drop rescheduling is only supported within the same provider's calendar in this view.
- **Task List**: A checklist of action items associated with a specific appointment type or patient milestone, used to guide staff through required steps at key points in the patient journey.
- **Taxonomy Code**: An optional field on a provider record for the provider's specialty/taxonomy code, used in insurance claims.
- **Templated Hours**: System-level schedule templates that can be applied to automatically populate provider availability.
- **Test Location**: An Office Info flag that designates a location as a test or sandbox environment rather than a live production location.
- **Time Zone**: An Office Info setting defining the local time zone for an office location; incorrect settings can corrupt appointment times and must be changed by ChiroHD support only.
- **Transaction Modal / Transaction Module**: The UI panel used to add charges, services, inventory items, and payments to a patient's ledger; accessible from multiple entry points in the system.
- **Transfer**: A Transaction Module feature that allows a payment to be split or redirected to a different patient's account within the same transaction.
- **Treated Symbol**: A calendar icon indicating that a provider has entered a SOAP note for a patient visit; does not necessarily indicate that payment is required.
- **Trigger**: An automated rule in ChiroHD that fires when specific criteria are met (e.g., appointment type = "Day Two" AND status = "Arrived") to automatically perform an action such as attaching a Task List.

## U

- **Unknown Provider Error**: An error appearing on billing/claim sheets when a calendar user has not been correctly linked to a provider record in the Providers settings.

## V

- **Visit Tracker**: A counter attached to a patient's account that tracks the number of adjustments rendered against a care plan target, with alerts triggered at defined milestones.

## W

- **Walk-In (Icon)**: A calendar appointment icon displaying a small figure to indicate that a patient arrived as a walk-in rather than a scheduled appointment.
- **Write-Off Amount**: The difference between the standard service rate and the discounted package rate applied to a patient's ledger; represents the amount forgiven as part of a care package discount.
- **Write-Off Reason**: A label applied to a write-off for reporting and auditing context (e.g., "Prepay," "Professional Write-Off"); selected on both the Default Charges tab and within fee schedules.
