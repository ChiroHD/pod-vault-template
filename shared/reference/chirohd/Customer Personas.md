## Overview

ChiroHD serves chiropractic practices. Three primary personas interact with the platform daily. Understanding their pain points and workflows is critical for prioritization.

> **Note:** These are initial personas based on pre-start research. Validate and refine through customer discovery interviews in weeks 1-4.

---

## Persona 1: Practice Owner

**Who:** Chiropractor who owns the practice. Often also the primary clinical provider.

**Goals:**
- Grow the practice (new patients, retention, revenue)
- Minimize time spent on admin/documentation
- See clear ROI from software investment
- Stay compliant without thinking about compliance

**Pain Points:**
- Documentation burden eats into patient time and personal life
- Hard to see practice health metrics at a glance
- Software changes disrupt established workflows
- Billing complexity and claims denials reduce revenue

**Relationship with ChiroHD:**
- Decision maker for purchasing
- Evaluates based on: "Does this save me time and make me money?"
- Change-resistant — has built workflows around current tools
- Vocal about frustrations, especially documentation time

**Key Insight:** Practice owners think in terms of time and money. Every feature must answer: "How does this give me more of either?"

---

## Persona 2: Clinical Provider (Associate Chiropractor)

**Who:** Employed chiropractor or associate. Uses the EHR daily for clinical documentation.

**Goals:**
- Document patient visits accurately and quickly
- Focus on patient care, not software
- Access patient history easily during appointments
- Complete notes before leaving for the day

**Pain Points:**
- SOAP note documentation is repetitive and time-consuming
- High-frequency visit model (patients 2-3x/week) multiplies documentation burden
- Template rigidity — doesn't match their specific documentation style
- Switching between screens during patient encounters

**Relationship with ChiroHD:**
- Primary daily user of clinical features
- Doesn't choose the software — uses what the practice owner selected
- Satisfaction directly impacts practice retention
- Workflow efficiency is their #1 priority

**Key Insight:** Chiropractic visits are high-frequency and short (15-20 min). Documentation must be faster than the visit, or it becomes backlog. This is where ambient AI documentation can be transformative.

---

## Persona 3: Billing / Admin Staff

**Who:** Front desk, billing coordinator, office manager. Handles scheduling, insurance, claims.

**Goals:**
- Process claims accurately and quickly
- Minimize claim denials and rework
- Keep the schedule full and organized
- Handle patient communication efficiently

**Pain Points:**
- Disconnect between clinical documentation and billing codes
- Manual data entry and reconciliation
- Insurance company requirements change frequently
- Patient communication is fragmented across phone, email, text

**Relationship with ChiroHD:**
- Heavy daily user of scheduling, billing, and patient engagement features
- Often the most technically proficient user in the practice
- Catches issues that providers miss (missing documentation → claim denial)
- Frustration with billing features directly impacts practice revenue

**Key Insight:** Billing staff are the bridge between clinical work and revenue. When SOAP notes don't cleanly translate to billing codes, they do the manual work to fix it. Automating this connection = huge value.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

| Theme | Practice Owner | Clinical Provider | Billing/Admin |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Documentation burden** | Hates it | Drowning in it | Deals with consequences |
| **AI opportunity** | "Will it save me time?" | "Will it write my notes?" | "Will it reduce denials?" |
| **Change tolerance** | Low | Medium | Medium-High |
| **Feature evaluation** | ROI-driven | Workflow-driven | Accuracy-driven |
